Vitae

Overview

Vitae is a responsive mobile app to help busy people build better habits and improve their overall health and well-being.

Roles

  • UX Researcher
  • UX Designer

Process

Process

My process is interative, constantly identifying problems and developing solutions to create a better product while keeping users and their needs in mind.

Problem

Long term improvement of physical and mental health requires consistent effort. People have multiple demands on their time and energy that make forming the habits that lead to positive health outcomes a daunting task.

Solution

Since mental and physical well-being go hand in hand, Vitae allows users to select or create habits of both mind and body to track in order to incrementally build healthier lifestyles. Vitae also offers suggested activities to support their growth based on their unique needs and preferences.

01. Building Empathy

Surveys & Interviews

My research goals were to get a better understanding of the following:

  • What are barriers to health and wellness?
  • What are users' health priorities?
  • What features do users prefer in health and wellness apps?

First, I deployed a survey and gathered 33 responses that gave me a general feel for user needs. Based on these responses, I then conducted several user interviews to get detailed qualitative data to create user personas that would guide my work moving forward.

02. Defining the Problem

Personas

I synthesized my research findings via affinity mapping to create personas reflective of potential Vitae users; this helped me to put a face to my problem statement:

Lily needs a way to reduce stress and lose weight so she can live a more balanced, healthier life.We will know this to be true when she reaches a two-week habit streak for both physical and mental activities while using the Vitae app.

03. Ideating Solutions

User Journey Maps, User Flows, Information Architecture, and Wireframes

Once I framed the design problem from the point of view of the user, I began to ideate potential solutions for users like Lily. User journey maps and user flows helped me consider what the experience would feel like from Lily's perspective.

I also began to conceptualize what the app would look like by developing the information architecture, which I tested through card sorting. Preliminary sketches and low-fidelity wireframes were also developed at this phase.

04. Prototyping the Product

Mid-Fidelity Prototyping

Applying my prior research, I then created a tangible and functional prototype of Vitae for the purpose of usability testing and further refinement.

05. Testing & Collaboration

Usability & A/B Testing

After recruiting test participants, I tested the key features of Vitae. I organized the data into another affinity map and then compiled it into a rainbow sheet to identify how the app was supporting user needs and what areas needed improvement.

A/B testing also indicated a strong preference for a green color scheme as opposed to blue, since users found blue to elicit coldness and emotional detachment, whereas green inspired feelings of vitality and calm.

Collaborative Feedback

Luckily, I had the opportunity to receive constructive feedback on my project from other designers. They gave me many great ideas which really improved Vitae's overall look and feel.

I am also very grateful that I could return the favor for others. It allowed me to give back to my professional community as well as gain design inspiration from other talented professionals!

The Final Product

UI & High-fidelity Prototyping

Cycles of testing and iterative refinement helped Vitae become what is is today! I also applied UI fundamentals to create a pleasing experience that would help Lily and others users use small moments each day to create life-changing habits and happier lives.
